Essential Oil

Essential oils are often used in aromatherapy, a form of alternative medicine that employs plant extracts to support health and well-being. Essential oils are compounds extracted from plants. The oils capture the plant’s scent and flavor, or “essence.” Unique aromatic compounds give each essential oil its characteristic essence.

Essential oils are obtained through distillation (via steam and/or water) or mechanical methods, such as cold pressing. Once the aromatic chemicals have been extracted, they are combined with a carrier oil to create a product that’s ready for use.

Medicated oil and synthetic essential oil

Medicated oils usually contain ingredients like menthol and methyl salicylate. Some medicated oils also contain active ingredients like natural eucalyptus oil, mint oil and rose oil for fragrance purposes. They are commonly used externally and applied to the skin for their soothing effects.

Synthetic Essential Oils are prepared instrumentally from plants and chemicals. They are made by different chemical formulation, and further converted into the oil form by different mechanical processes. They contact artificial preservatives which are not harmful, but give a longer shelf life than any essential oil..

Mineral oil Mineral oil is a clear odorless liquid that has been used routinely for many decades in a wide variety of cosmetics and personal care products.
Glycerin (sometimes called glycerol) Glycerin used in cosmetics and personal care products can be obtained from natural sources (e.g., soybeans, cane, or corn syrup sugar) or manufactured synthetically. Glycerin is used safely in numerous cosmetics and personal care products such as soaps, toothpaste, shaving cream, and skin/hair care products to provide smoothness and lubrication.  Other reported functions for glycerin include use as a fragrance ingredient, denaturant, hair conditioning agent, oral care agent, skin conditioning agent— humectant, skin protectant, oral health care drug, and viscosity decreasing agent. Glycerin is widely used in the food industry and in pharmaceutical (i.e., drug) formulations. In food and beverages, glycerin serves as a humectant, a solvent, and a sweetener and may help with preservation.
Lanolin Lanolin and its related ingredients moisturize the skin, hair and nails. These ingredients act as a lubricants on the skin surface, which gives the skin a soft, smooth appearance. Lanolin helps to form emulsions and blends well with nearly all other substances used in cosmetics and personal care products. Lanolin also possesses adhesive characteristics The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) permits Lanolin to be used in Over-the-Counter (OTC) drugs that protect the skin, and in OTC drugs that protect the anorectal area. Lanolin is also permitted to be used as an opthalmic emollient in OTC ophthalmic drug products. In food, FDA permits the use of Lanolin as an softener in chewing gum. 
Tocopherol, or vitamin E Tocopherol, or vitamin E, a fat-soluble vitamin is a naturally occuring antioxidant which can be isolated from vegetable oil.  In cosmetics and personal care products, Tocopherol and other ingredients made from Tocopherol, including Tocopherol esters are used in the formulation of lipstick, eye shadow, blushers, face powders and foundations, moisturizers, skin care products, bath soaps and detergents, hair conditioners, and many other products.

Carbonate powder
Calcium Carbonate, Magnesium Carbonate, Potassium Carbonate and Zinc Carbonate are all salts of carbonic acid. All of these ingredients are white powders. These carbonate salts are used in bath products, makeup products, personal cleanliness products, shaving products, oral care products and skin and hair care products.

ORYZA SATIVA POWDER (rice powder)
Oryza Sativa Powder is a natural ingredient commonly used in cosmetic formulations. It is also known as rice powder and is obtained by finely grinding the Oryza Sativa (rice) grain. The powder is rich in nutrients and antioxidants, making it popular in skincare products.

It works to improve the skin's appearance by reducing wrinkles, fine lines, and dark spots. It helps to brighten the skin and give it a more youthful appearance. Oryza Sativa Powder appears as a fine white powder, which makes it easy to incorporate into a wide range of cosmetic products. It is commonly used in facial masks, exfoliants, and other skincare products to help improve the overall texture and appearance of the skin.

Origin - Oryza Sativa Powder is produced by grinding the grains of the Oryza Sativa plant, commonly known as rice, into a fine powder. The process involves removing the outer husk of the rice grains and then grinding the remaining inner part into a smooth powder.
